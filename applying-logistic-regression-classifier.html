It's confirmed that ratio for our class representation is - 0:1 = 3:1. So it is imbalanced, there are tactics to handle this which you can learn about here - https://machinelearningmastery.com/tactics-to-combat-imbalanced-classes-in-your-machine-learning-dataset/ . As it is itself a wider topic and i myself have not been able to grasp it yet(specifically SMOTE) i'm not able to talk about it here. But there is way to penalise the over-represented class, which is sometimes a good way to handle this problem. For example if we were classifying cancer, a False-Positive(patient doesn't have cancer but predited a positive) or False-Negative (patient has cancer but predited a negative) may result in drastic effect, and where we can take chance with a false-positive rather false-negative(patient stays without treatment due to wrong prediction), in this case we would like to penalise classes accordingly. Let's have a look:-
